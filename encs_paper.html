Augmented Beauty

This paper was an assignment for my ENCS 393 (Social and Ethical Dimensions of Information and Communication Technologies) course at Concordia. We were instructed to explore an ethical dilemma in technology and analyse it through the lens of various ethical viewpoints.

Read Paper

Summary

My paper discusses the impact of augmented reality (AR) filters, specifically beauty-focused ones, on social media users' self-esteem. AR filters can simulate plastic surgery by virtually altering the 3-dimensional physical properties of a user's face, which could lead to unrealistic beauty standards and negatively affect one's mental health. Therefore, one might jump to the conclusion that we should ban such effects. However, digital creators use these same features to create innovative experiences, like making the user look like a cartoon, an inhuman creature, or something new entirely. Where do we draw the line between simulating plastic surgery and harmless fun? Should we even limit the creator’s or user’s freedom of self expression? I explore both sides and offer possible solutions and areas of further research.
