Black & White

I designed an album cover using Photoshop, based on a portrait I photographed and edited in Lightroom.

Concept

Concept

I designed album art for my friend, nicknamed Hori. His music explores themes of racial discrimination and injustice. This image is for a song titled "Black & White"

I designed album art for my friend, nicknamed Hori. His music explores themes of racial discrimination and injustice. This image is for a song titled "Black & White"

Original Photo

Original Photo

My friends were shooting a music video. I had just started pursuing photography more seriously, so I used a 2005 point and shoot to get supporting shots. We had a concept of a man in a high end car yet wearing a ski mask as if he's still on the run.

My friends were shooting a music video. I had just started pursuing photography more seriously, so I used a 2005 point and shoot to get supporting shots. We had a concept of a man in a high end car yet wearing a ski mask as if he's still on the run.

Color Edit

Color Edit

Although the concept was cool, my skills were undeveloped at the time. I returned to the photo over a year later with the ability to fix it up into album art. I came up with the idea to isolate Hori's skin tone to bring the viewer's attention to it. I loved the hint of color poking through the black ski mask.

Expanded

Expanded

I also hadn't shot the image with the intention of making an album cover. So, it didn't have the correct aspect ratio, and cropping it would've ruined the composition. I roughly expanded the top using Photoshop's retouch tools and later blurred it to make it into a square.

Final Image

Final Image

The image is meant to get the viewer to question why Hori is wearing a ski mask, ducking in a Tesla. It's monochrome to fit the song name. His skintone being highlighted maintains a personal touch. The song title initially refers to colors, but within the lyrics it refers to race and moral dichotomies.
